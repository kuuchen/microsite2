about Venice Biannale 2018

This years’ curators of the 16th International Architecture Exhibition, Yvonne Farrell and Shelley McNamara titled the 16th International Architecture Exhibition Freespace, and explained their choice with the following words:

Freespace describes a generosity of spirit and a sense of humanity at the core of architecture's agenda, focusing on the quality of space itself.

Freespace focuses on architecture’s ability to provide free and additional spatial gifts to those who use it and on its ability to address the unspoken wishes of strangers.

Freespace celebrates architecture’s capacity to find additional and unexpected generosity in each project - even within the most private, defensive, exclusive or commercially restricted conditions.

about "No More Free Space?", Singapore Pavilion

The Singapore Pavilion addresses this year’s biennale titled ‘freespace’ by asking if there is really ‘no more free space?’ in the island country, as a testimony of how the creative freedom of mind has the power to turn space constraints into a myriad of alternative possibilities.

Curated by the Singapore University of Technology and Design (SUTD) in collaboration with the National University of Singapore’s Department of Architecture (NUS), the exhibition features 12 Singapore-based projects and an immersive centrepiece that tells the story of how, in spite of the lack of free space, Singapore-based architects, urban planners and place-makers have creatively found ways to bring delightful free spaces to the city’s everyday life. The projects on show tap into social capital in order to bring joy and connect people to the larger community. The Singapore Pavilion is commissioned by the DesignSingapore Council (DSG) of the Ministry of Communications and Information, and the Urban Redevelopment Authority (URA).

Complete with lights, scent, sounds and images of Singapore, the pavilion invites visitors to experience the spaces within the cloud and interact with the installation through a series of scented, translucent ceramic end-pieces that glow when they sense proximity. The pavilion is an example of a resourceful, unexpected free space, designed to reach beyond physical constraints and instead, engage with the qualities of space-making that celebrate generosity and humanity through our senses.

the Pavilion's centrepiece

The Pavilion’s centrepiece features an immersive, multi-sensory installation, a suspended transparent cloud made of handcrafted acrylic knots. The cloud is programmed to transmit light from above, shifting in color and intensity to complement projected videos of Singapore’s built environment below. By trapping, bending, and transmitting, the cloud creates an immersive environment and is a visual spectacle.

featured projects

The exhibition features 12 Singapore-based projects that showcase the resourcefulness of architects to turn constraints into possibilities. More than 400 times smaller than Italy, Singapore has turned its lack of space into an opportunity to re-imagine what a highly compact city can be.

No More Free Space? We...

...define space by volume not by area.

Lucky Shophouse by CHANG Architects
Type: Residential, Client: Private Client

We restored an old book store to become a residential house and, instead of maximising built-up areas, transformed the concrete backyard into a single-story extension and a garden, thus offering breathing spaces and green spaces for the family to enjoy.

...breathe in between.

T House by Ling Hao Architects
Type: Residential, Client: Private Client

We brought the garden, the sun, rain, and breeze into the house with a partially submerged yet open living room, where the occupants of the house can live together, grow plants and enjoy the outdoors indoor.

...live in the sky.

SkyTerrace @ Dawson by SCDA Architects Pte Ltd
Type: High Rise Residential, Client: Housing Development Board

We designed inter-connected but separate loft units in high-rise public housing to allow young families to live close to elderly parents, while retaining the privacy and independence of both parties.

...upcycle, repurpose, reuse.

Enabling Village by WOHA Architects Pte Ltd
Type: Institution, Client: SG Enable

We adapted a former vocational institute into an inclusive space – integrating education, work, training, retail and lifestyle – that connects people with disabilities to residents in nearby housing estates and to the wider community.

...borrow a pond.

Khoo Teck Puat Hospital by CPG Consultants Pte Ltd Type: Hospital, Client: National Healthcare Group

We integrated the neighbouring pond into the overall hospital design, and transformed the hospital into a waterfront health-promoting park for patients and residents in the vicinity to use for exercise, recreation, interaction and recuperation.

...undo the drain.

Bishan - Ang Mo Kio Park by Ramboll Studio Dreiseitl Pte Ltd Type: Public Space, Client: National Parks Board, Public Utilities Board

We turned a storm water canal back into a free- flowing river, which meanders through a park, to bring back biodiversity, and to bring people closer to water and let them interact with it, enjoy it, and cherish it.

...draw a line to connect the ridges.

Southern Ridges by RSP Architects Planners & Engineers Pte Ltd, IJP Corporation Ltd, UK, LOOK Architects Pte Ltd
Type: Public Space, Client: National Parks Board, Urban Redevelopment Authority

We connected five parks and gardens in the southern part of Singapore by picturesque bridges and pathways into a 10-kilometre stretch of green open spaces where visitors can enjoy the best panoramic view of the city, harbour and the Southern Islands.

...reclaim.

Marina Bay by ZARCH Collaborative Pte Ltd, CPG Consultants Pte Ltd, Grant Associates (UK), Wilkinson Eyre Architects (UK)
Type: Public Space

To prepare for the growth of the city centre, we have been reclaiming the city’s waterfront since the 1970s. The Marina Bay district is testament to this national effort and has become a top destination for business and recreation where large-scale events, such as i Light Marina Bay Light Art Festival, are held – reclaiming the waterfront and its public spaces for the community.

...let kids imagine.

The Caterpillar’s Cove by Lekker Architects Pte Ltd Type: Education, Client: NTUC First Campus

We reimagined a conventional office space into an adaptive open plan for young children to play and learn through exploration and self-regulation – a model for other early-childhood learning facilities.

...learn in the mall.

Library @ Orchard by Singapore Polytechnic, New Space Architects Pte Ltd, DIA Brand Consultants
Type: Library, Client: National Library Board

We conceived a more engaging public library in a shopping mall, using design -thinking to study the needs and interests of library users and the community.

...dine together at the void.

Goodlife! Makan by DP Architects Pte Ltd
Type: Community Space, Client: National Library Board

Using food as a connector, we converted an empty space below a public housing block into a communal kitchen to reconnect stay-alone seniors to the wider community through cooking and dining activities.

...occupy.

Community Living Room by Residents of Jurong East and Tampines, Singapore University of Technology and Design, National University of Singapore Type: Community Space, Client: Residents of Jurong East and Tampines

Residents of several public housing estates engaged in dialogues with town managers and designers, and collaboratively transformed unused corners into community living rooms and open farms for everyone.
